self.Code

Identification of sarcasm in news headline
Objective: Recognition of sarcasm in news headlines after training on labelled dataset using Deep Learning algorithm. A pre-trained uncased BERT model was used over which a final layer for classification was applied to achieve desired results. The model identifies sarcastic articles with an accuracy of 80%.

Python, NLTK, PyTorch, Transformer, BERT

Deduplicating news articles collected from various sources
Objective: Collecting news from multiple sources and clustering similar news together. The focus is on building systems that can collate latest news from various sources and identify articles that are conveying the same news to group them together.

Python, Gensim [Doc2Vec], BeautifulSoup

Abnormal Gait Detection by Classifying inertial sensor data using Transfer Learning
Objective: Identifying neurolological disorders by studying the pattern of different walking styles. Data was collected using sensors placed on subject's key areas to fetch the appropriate data. The data obtained from sensors was converted to images which was then fed to a pre-trained Inception-V3 model.

Python, Arduino, Tensorflow, Inception-V3

About Myself

Hello, my name is Tejul and I am passionate about building systems backed by data. I enjoy the process of learning and keep updating myself with new algorithms as well as technologies.

I developed my keen interest in building Machine Learning models back in 2016, while I was pursuing my undergraduate degree from University of Mumbai. In 2018, I joined CRISIL Limited, an S&P Global Company as an Associate and my first project was responsible for bringing an AI-revolution within the organization. A CNN model was trained to sort 200-500 news articles daily into its respective sectors and sub-sectors within seconds. With following years, I gained more experience particularly in the field of Natural Language Processing.

I am excited about the next step and look forward to an enriching experience at Northwestern University that will enable me to build on my mathematical and programming skills.

Know Me Better!

I began building my foundation for Machine Learning in 2016 with the help of multiple online MOOCS and personal projects. This was followed by a natural progression by working at Pragmatix Services as a Data Scientist Intern wherein with my first project I received the exposure of studying financial data and building models for identifying fraud credit card transactions for an International Bank.
